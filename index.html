SHOULD YOU TIP YOUR MOVERS?

There are so many questions, yet so little answers when it comes to relocation. This is especially true if we are moving to a colossal city like New York. Which neighborhood to choose? Who are the best real estate agents? Where to find a reliable moving company? Well, the best way to organize a successful and efficient relocation is to partner up with a local moving company. For example, Woodbridge township movers are your best option if you are moving to that part of New York. Hiring local movers is always a safer option because you can actually meet them in person and see if they tick all the right boxes considering the quality of services and professionalism. Some questions remain even when the move is over. Should you tip your movers? If yes, then how much? Luckily for you, we have a few ideas that could help you in this sense. Stay with us for more.

Why would you tip your movers?

The first question you might ask yourself is: Why would I tip the moving crew when I already paid the full price to the moving agency? Honestly, you don’t have to. This decision is entirely up to you. If you wish to show your gratitude for the hard and efficient work that the moving crew put in, tipping is a nice way to do that. Think of the situation where you were satisfied with the service in a restaurant or in a cab. The same applies to the moving services. On the other hand, you are not obliged to tip your movers, especially in the following situations:

  • Your movers were late
  • They were unprofessional and impolite
  • The movers damaged your property
  • They did a lousy job in general

These are all the traits of amateur moving companies and in these cases instead of tipping, you should consider filing a claim against the company. This is why you should invest some time to discover reputable Manhattan movers and have a stress-free relocation.

How much should you tip your movers?

Again this is completely up to you, but there are some general rules in the moving industry. If your movers did a satisfactory job, tipping 4-6$ per hour is considered ok. This translates to around 40$ per day of work. In case you were extremely happy with moving the crew’s effort then you can raise the tips to 7-9$ per hour. Or you can give them 20% of the total price. But remember that long-distance moves are pricier so 20%, in that case, might not be such a good idea.

You don’t have to tip your movers to show your gratitude. There are other ways to do it.

Other ways to show your gratitude

Instead of tipping, you can do some of the following:

  • Provide refreshments and some snacks
  • Prepare clean towels and soap
  • Leave a written feedback

All things considered, just trust your instincts, and at the end of the day, you will know if you should tip your movers and how much. Have a safe relocation.
